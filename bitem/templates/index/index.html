Welcome to bITEM,

the web application to help you unveil the stories of museum objects.

bITEM sees the fascinating story behind every museum object, waiting to be discovered. Our interactive platform, created by a multidisciplinary team, invites you to engage with these stories through compelling features. Designed for anyone with a curiosity about cultural heritage, bITEM highlights the captivating biographies of significant artifacts and specimens, making history accessible and enjoyable for all.

Join us on bITEM and embark on a digital adventure through time. Discover the stories behind museum artifacts and see how they are connected in ways you never imagined.

Case Studies

Venus von Willendorf
On August 7, 1908, during an excavation led by Josef Szombathy, one of the most famous archaeological discoveries was made: the Venus of Willendorf. This remarkable ca. 29,500 years old figurine was unearthed in Willendorf, Lower Austria, in the picturesque Wachau Valley. This case study delves into the fascinating history and research of the Venus of Willendorf, tracing her journey from the moment she was found to her significance in the present day.
Novara Expedition
On 30 April 1857, the Austrian navy set sail on its first circumnavigation of the globe as part of the Novara Expedition. This pioneering voyage inspired the scientific community of the time and led to important discoveries, for example on Saint Paul's Island, the Nicobar Islands and in New Zealand. The collected botanical, zoological and ethnological exhibits enriched numerous museums and laid the foundation for the Natural History Museum Vienna. This case study highlights the remarkable achievements and lasting legacy of the Novara Expedition.
Mitterhof
Surrounding the Mitterhof farm in Lower Austria lies a small but historically fascinating area with traces of settlement dating back to the Early Bronze Age and onwards. This area, located at the Czech border, experienced various phases of human history, including the Middle Bronze Age, the Latène period, the Marcomannic Wars and the early to late Middle Ages. Of particular interest are several Bronze Age and 5th century burials and the so-called ‘Runde Berg’, which may have been used both as a prehistoric burial mound and a medieval motte-and-bailey castle. This case study reveals the fascinating history and changes to the Mitterhof site.
